Bug Reporter Characterization Analysis

January 7, 2026 • Nimrod Fisher | AI Analytics Hub

Executive Summary

Key Finding: Bug reporters are high-value power users who generate 28-32% more revenue and are 15.6% more active than other users. They show no elevated churn risk.

This analysis examined 22 bug tickets from 21 unique users across 20 accounts (Dec 2024 - Jun 2025). Bug reports should be treated as engagement signals from premium customers, not churn warnings.

Detailed Findings:

1. Bug Reporters Are Power Users
They generate 15.6% more events (11.14 vs 9.64 avg) and remain active slightly longer (72 vs 68 days activity span).
2. High-Value Customers
Bug-reporting accounts generate 28-32% more revenue ($210.59 vs $164.21 avg MRR) with 8% longer subscription tenure.
3. No Churn Risk Signal
Canceled subscription rates are nearly identical (0.71 vs 0.69), indicating bug reports are NOT churn warnings.
4. Widespread Distribution
Bug reporting is not concentrated - only 2 accounts (4%) have multiple bug tickets. Most accounts (90%) report once.
5. No Feature-Specific Pattern
Bug reporters perform all event types at similar rates to other users (all overrepresentation ratios < 0.13).
Recommendations

Actionable Next Steps:

  1. Prioritize bug fixes for high-MRR accounts ($150+) - They generate 28% more revenue and represent valuable customers.
  2. Segment CSM coverage for bug-reporting accounts - $210 avg MRR warrants dedicated support and proactive outreach.
  3. DO NOT trigger churn campaigns - Bug reports are engagement signals, not churn warnings.
  4. Expand QA for power user workflows - Power users encounter edge cases; broad testing needed.
  5. Investigate 2 multi-bug accounts individually - Only 2 accounts have 2+ bugs; may indicate specific issues.
